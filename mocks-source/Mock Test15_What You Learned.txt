🧠 What You Learned

These are the key facts and topics reinforced in this mock test. Review them to strengthen your knowledge before taking another mock test.


* 🏴 **St George** → Patron Saint of **England**, represented by the **Rose**
* 🏞 **Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park** → Located in **Scotland**
* ⛪ **Established Churches** → **Wales has no established church**, just like Northern Ireland
* 📖 **Geoffrey Chaucer** → Author of **The Canterbury Tales**, one of England's greatest literary works
* 📦 **Boxing Day** → Celebrated on **26 December**, the day after Christmas
* 🏦 **Bank Holidays** → Official UK public holidays when banks and many businesses close
* 🗳 **Women's Voting Rights** → Women over **30** gained the vote in **1918** following their contribution during the **First World War**
* 🚗 **Driving Laws** → A **valid driving licence** is required to drive legally in the UK
* 🎭 **Edinburgh Fringe Festival** → The world's largest arts festival, held every summer in **Edinburgh**
* 🏛 **Welsh Senedd** → The Welsh Parliament consists of **60 Members (MSs)**
* 👦 **Youth Courts** → Protect the identity of young offenders by preventing publication of their names and photographs
* 🎼 **Sir Edward Elgar** → Composer of **Pomp and Circumstance**, traditionally performed at the **Last Night of the Proms**
* 💷 **British Banknotes** → Standard Bank of England notes are **£5, £10, £20 and £50** – there is **no £25 note**
* 🏇 **Scottish Grand National** → Annual horse race held at **Ayr**, Scotland
* 👑 **Constitutional Monarchy** → Established after the **Glorious Revolution (1688)** and the **Bill of Rights (1689)**
* 🚢 **Spanish Armada (1588)** → Fleet sent by **Spain** and defeated during the reign of **Elizabeth I**
* 📜 **Habeas Corpus Act (1679)** → Guarantees every prisoner the **right to a court hearing** and protection from unlawful imprisonment
* 👩 **Emmeline Pankhurst** → Leader of the **Suffragette movement**, campaigning for women's right to vote
* 🎻 **The Proms** → An **eight-week summer season of orchestral classical music concerts**, mainly at the Royal Albert Hall
* 🍎 **Sir Isaac Newton** → Explained the **law of gravity** and the laws of motion
* 🌍 **First World War** → Ended in **1918**, commemorated each year on **Armistice Day (11 November)**
* 🧱 **Hadrian's Wall** → Built by **Emperor Hadrian** to defend Roman Britain from northern tribes
* 🚣 **The Boat Race** → Annual rowing competition between the **University of Oxford** and the **University of Cambridge**
* 📜 **UK Constitution** → The United Kingdom has an **unwritten constitution**, based on laws, conventions and historic documents rather than a single written text
