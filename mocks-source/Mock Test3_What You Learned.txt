🧠 What You Learned

These are the key facts and topics reinforced in this mock test. Review them to strengthen your knowledge before taking another mock test.

* 👑 **Crown Dependencies** → The **Isle of Man**, Jersey and Guernsey are Crown Dependencies, not part of the UK
* 📺 **BBC** → Publicly funded but **independent of government** and required to provide impartial news
* 🏛 **Northern Ireland Assembly** → Has **90 MLAs** and legislates on devolved matters such as health and education
* ⚓ **Admiral Nelson** → Led the British fleet and died at the **Battle of Trafalgar (1805)**
* 🪙 **Iron Age Britain** → The first British coins were inscribed with the names of **Iron Age kings**
* 🏘 **Local Government** → Local councils are democratically elected to provide community services
* 🎨 **Tate Galleries** → Tate Britain and Tate Modern are located in **London**
* 📖 **King James Bible** → Also known as the **Authorised Version**, published during the reign of James I
* 🎸 **Swinging Sixties** → A period of growth in British fashion, pop music and cinema
* 🇫🇷 **Huguenots** → French Protestants who settled in England between 1680 and 1720
* 🎾 **Wimbledon** → The world's oldest and most famous tennis tournament
* 🏛 **Members of Parliament (MPs)** → Can be contacted at Westminster, constituency offices or local surgeries
* 😂 **April Fool's Day** → Celebrated on **1 April** with practical jokes until midday
* 🏴 **Robert the Bruce** → Defeated the English at the **Battle of Bannockburn (1314)**
* 🌐 **Sir Tim Berners-Lee** → Invented the **World Wide Web**
* 👨‍💼 **Civil Service** → Civil servants are appointed on **merit** and remain politically neutral
* 🚢 **Dunkirk Spirit** → Refers to the evacuation of Allied soldiers from France during **World War II (1940)**
* ⚖ **Jury Service** → Available to people aged **18–70** who are on the electoral register
* 🇬🇧 **Responsibilities of Citizens** → Respect others, obey the law and look after yourself, your family and your community
* 🏅 **London 2012 Olympics** → The UK hosted the Summer Olympic Games for the third time
* 👑 **Charles I** → Believed in the **Divine Right of Kings** and ruled without Parliament, leading to the English Civil War
* 🇬🇧 **Union Jack** → The national flag of the United Kingdom, combining the crosses of St George, St Andrew and St Patrick
* 🏆 **Winston Churchill** → Voted the **Greatest Briton** in 2002 for his leadership during the Second World War
* 👩 **Equality in Modern Britain** → Women make up about **half of the UK workforce**
