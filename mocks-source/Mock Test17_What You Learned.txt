🧠 What You Learned

These are the key facts and topics reinforced in this mock test. Review them to strengthen your knowledge before taking another mock test.

* 🌍 **Second World War (1939)** → Began when **Germany invaded Poland**, prompting Britain and France to declare war
* 🌿 **National Flower of Scotland** → The **Thistle**, closely associated with **St Andrew**
* 👮 **Police Complaints** → Can be made through **multiple official routes**, including independent complaints bodies
* 🏛 **Home Secretary** → Responsible for **crime, policing and immigration**
* 🌼 **St David's Day** → Celebrated on **1 March**, honouring the patron saint of Wales
* 👸 **Mary, Queen of Scots** → Executed in **1587** after 20 years of imprisonment
* 🧬 **Francis Crick** → Co-discovered the **structure of the DNA molecule**
* 💷 **Scottish and Northern Irish Banknotes** → Valid throughout the **United Kingdom**
* 🏞 **National Parks** → There are **15 National Parks** across England, Wales and Scotland
* 🏝 **Great Britain** → Includes **England, Scotland and Wales**, but **not Northern Ireland**
* 🐶 **Dog Collar Law** → Dogs in public must display the **owner's name and address** on their collar
* ✝ **Good Friday** → Commemorates the **death of Jesus Christ**, before Easter Sunday
* 🗳 **Members of Parliament (MPs)** → Represent everyone living in their **constituency**
* 📜 **Magna Carta (1215)** → Limited the **King's power** and established the principle that everyone is subject to the law
* 🚫 **Female Genital Mutilation (FGM)** → Illegal in the UK and treated as a **serious criminal offence**
* 👩 **Women's Voting Rights (1918)** → Women over **30** gained the right to vote and stand for Parliament
* 🌍 **British Overseas Territories** → **St Helena** and the **Falkland Islands** are linked to the UK but are **not part of it**
* 🚢 **Spanish Armada (1588)** → Defeated by England during the reign of **Queen Elizabeth I**
* 🌺 **The Cenotaph** → Designed by **Sir Edwin Lutyens** as Britain's national war memorial
* 🏛 **Welsh Senedd** → Members are elected **every four years**
* 🎵 **The Swinging Sixties** → The **Beatles** and **Rolling Stones** became world-famous while major social reforms took place
* 📚 **Booker Prize** → Prestigious award recognising excellence in **literature**
* 🗳 **By-election** → Held when an **MP dies or resigns**, allowing one constituency to elect a replacement
* 🎨 **Turner Prize** → One of Europe's leading awards for **contemporary visual art**
