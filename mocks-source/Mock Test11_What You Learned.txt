🧠 What You Learned

These are the key facts and topics reinforced in this mock test. Review them to strengthen your knowledge before taking another mock test.


* 👮 **Home Secretary** → Responsible for **crime, policing and immigration**

* ⛪ **House of Lords** → Senior **Church of England bishops (Lords Spiritual)** sit alongside peers
* 🐕 **Dog Ownership Laws** → Dogs in public places must wear a **collar displaying the owner's name and address**
* 🗳 **Women's Voting Rights (1918)** → Women over **30** gained the right to vote following their contribution during the **First World War**
* 🇮🇪 **Republic of Ireland** → Ireland officially became a **republic in 1949**
* 🌾 **First Farmers in Britain** → Arrived around **6,000 years ago**, with ancestors from **south-east Europe**
* 🎨 **David Hockney** → One of Britain's most influential **Pop Art** artists
* 🇬🇧 **Winston Churchill** → Inspirational Prime Minister who led Britain during **most of the Second World War**
* ⚔ **Julius Caesar** → Led the **first Roman invasion of Britain** in **55 BC**
* 👑 **King Alfred the Great** → United the Anglo-Saxon kingdoms and defeated the **Vikings**
* 📜 **Magna Carta (1215)** → Limited the King's powers and laid the foundations of the **rule of law**
* 💷 **Northern Ireland Banknotes** → Valid throughout the **United Kingdom**
* 🎭 **William Shakespeare** → Wrote **A Midsummer Night's Dream**, one of Britain's most famous plays
* ⚖ **Jury System** → A jury in England, Wales and Northern Ireland consists of **12 members**
* 🪔 **Diwali** → Celebrated by **Hindus and Sikhs**
* ✝ **Good Friday** → Commemorates the **death of Jesus Christ**, before Easter Sunday
* ☘ **St Patrick's Day** → **17 March** is a public holiday in **Northern Ireland**
* 🏰 **Norman Conquest (1066)** → The **last successful foreign invasion** of England
* 💎 **Crown Jewels** → Kept at the **Tower of London**
* ⛳ **Golf** → Modern golf originated in **15th-century Scotland**
* 🖨 **William Caxton** → Introduced the **printing press** to England
* ⚖ **Magistrates' Court** → Handles most **minor criminal cases** in England, Wales and Northern Ireland
* 📺 **John Logie Baird** → Scottish inventor who developed **television**
