Q1. As a British citizen, you should: (choose two)

❌ Vote in all elections

✅ Respect and obey the law

❌ Take part in religious activities

✅ Look after the area in which you live and the environment

Explanation: As a British citizen you should respect and obey the law and look after the area in which you live and the environment.

🧠 Memory Tip

This is a SELECT TWO question.

Know why each option is right or wrong:

❌ Vote in all elections → Voting is a right, not a legal responsibility. You are encouraged to vote, but you are not legally required to do so.
✅ Respect and obey the law → One of the key responsibilities of every British citizen and permanent resident.
❌ Take part in religious activities → The UK has freedom of religion. You may follow any religion or none at all.
✅ Look after the area in which you live and the environment → Another important responsibility highlighted in the handbook.

Remember the key responsibilities:

⚖ Respect and obey the law

Q7. Which of the following is an example of criminal offence?

❌ Smoking in public places where it is not allowed

❌ Disputes about faulty goods or services

❌ Disputes about an unfair dismissal in the workplace

❌ A dispute between the landlord and the tenants who have not paid the rent

✅ Smoking in public places where it is not allowed

Explanation: Smoking in public places. It is against the law to smoke tobacco products in nearly every enclosed public place in the UK. There are signs displayed to tell you where you cannot smoke.

🧠 Memory Tip

Know why each option is correct or incorrect:

✅ Smoking in public places where it is not allowed → Correct. Smoking in enclosed public places where prohibited is a criminal offence.
❌ Disputes about faulty goods or services → A civil law matter between a customer and a business.
❌ Disputes about an unfair dismissal in the workplace → An employment (civil) law issue.
❌ A dispute between the landlord and the tenants who have not paid the rent → A civil law housing dispute.

Remember the difference:

🚔 Criminal Law → Crimes against society (e.g. theft, assault, illegal smoking, carrying offensive weapons).
⚖ Civil Law → Disputes between individuals or organisations.
Quick Memory Rule

🚔 Criminal Law → Breaking the Law

⚖ Civil Law → Solving Disputes
