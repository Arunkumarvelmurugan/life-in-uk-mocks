🧠 What You Learned

These are the key facts and topics reinforced in this mock test. Review them to strengthen your knowledge before taking another mock test.


* ⚔ **Battle of Waterloo (1815)** → The **last battle between Great Britain and France**, where the Duke of Wellington defeated Napoleon
* 🌼 **National Flower of Wales** → The **Daffodil**, closely associated with **St David**
* 🔥 **Great Fire of London (1666)** → Destroyed much of London during the reign of **Charles II**, leading to the rebuilding of St Paul's Cathedral
* 🗳 **General Elections** → Held at least **every five years** to elect Members of Parliament
* 💷 **Scottish Banknotes** → Issued in Scotland and **valid throughout the United Kingdom**
* 🏛 **The National Trust** → Charity that preserves the UK's **historic buildings, coastline and countryside**
* 🏝 **Great Britain** → Consists of **England, Scotland and Wales**, excluding Northern Ireland
* 👑 **Boudicca** → Queen of the **Iceni tribe** who led a rebellion against the Romans
* ⚔ **Winston Churchill** → Voted the **Greatest Briton of All Time** in **2002**
* 🏛 **UK Parliament** → Made up of the **House of Commons** and the **House of Lords**
* 🗳 **Equal Voting Rights (1928)** → Women gained the **right to vote at the same age as men (21)**
* 👑 **Head of the Church of England** → The **Monarch** serves as the Supreme Governor
* 🏇 **Grand National** → One of the world's most famous **horse racing** events
* 📖 **The Canterbury Tales** → A collection of **poems** written by **Geoffrey Chaucer**
* 🎄 **Christmas Day** → Celebrated on **25 December**, marking the birth of Jesus Christ
* ⚔ **English Civil War** → **Cavaliers** supported King Charles I, while **Roundheads** supported Parliament
* 🧵 **Bayeux Tapestry** → Commemorates the **Battle of Hastings (1066)** and William the Conqueror's victory
* 🏰 **Tower of London** → Site of **Anne Boleyn's execution** and home to the **Crown Jewels**
* ⚖ **Criminal Offences** → Include **racial crime** and smoking in prohibited public places
* 🎬 **Charlie Chaplin** → World-famous silent film actor best known for his character **The Tramp**
* 🏛 **Standing as an MP** → Anyone aged **18 or over** can stand for election to Parliament
* 📡 **Sir Robert Watson-Watt** → Developed **radar**, which played a crucial role during the Second World War
* 🏠 **10 Downing Street** → Official residence and office of the **UK Prime Minister**
* 📺📻 **Election Broadcasting Rules** → **Television and radio** must provide **balanced political coverage** before elections
