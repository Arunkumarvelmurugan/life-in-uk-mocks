Q1. Which of the following is not a constitutional institution?

The Parliament

The cabinet

The armed forces ✅

The police

Explanation: Constitutional institutions in the UK include: the monarchy, the Parliament (the House of Commons and the House of Lords), the Prime Minister, the cabinet, the judiciary (courts), the police, the civil service and the local government. The armed forces are not a constitutional institution.

Q10. What are TWO responsibilities of the monarch?

To appoint the members of the cabinet

To advise, warn and encourage the Prime Minister's decisions on government policies ✅

To debate important national issues

To appoint the government ✅

Explanation: The monarch does not rule the country but appoints the government, which the people have chosen in a democratic election. The monarch also has regular meetings with the Prime Minister and can advise, warn and encourage, but the decisions on government policies are made by the Prime Minister and cabinet.
