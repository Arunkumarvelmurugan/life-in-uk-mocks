🧠 What You Learned

These are the key facts and topics reinforced in this mock test. Review them to strengthen your knowledge before taking another mock test.

* 🕊 **Fundamental British Values** → Individual liberty is one of the core principles of British life, alongside democracy, the rule of law, and mutual respect and tolerance
* ⛪ **Established Churches** → England has an established Church, while Wales and Northern Ireland do not
* 🎆 **Hogmanay** → Scotland's traditional celebration of **New Year's Eve (31 December)**
* 👑 **Henry VIII & the Church of England** → Henry VIII established the Church of England after the Pope refused to grant a divorce from Catherine of Aragon
* ⚔ **Hundred Years' War** → The long conflict between England and France during the Middle Ages
* 🏴 **Bonnie Prince Charlie** → Led the Jacobite Rising of **1745** with support from Scottish Highland clans
* 👮 **Countering Extremism** → Suspicious extremist or terrorist activity should be reported to the **local police**
* 🤝 **National Citizen Service (NCS)** → An optional programme for 16–17-year-olds that develops leadership, teamwork and community involvement
* 🏰 **Tower of London** → Beefeaters (Yeoman Warders) serve as ceremonial guards and official tour guides
* ✈ **Concorde** → The world's only supersonic commercial airliner, jointly developed by **Britain and France**
* 🏞 **Snowdonia (Eryri)** → Famous national park located in **Wales**
* 🔍 **Sir Arthur Conan Doyle** → Creator of the famous detective **Sherlock Holmes**
* 🌍 **Second World War** → Germany invaded **Poland in 1939**, marking the beginning of WWII
* 🐉 **St David** → Patron Saint of **Wales**
* 🏞 **Lake District** → The largest National Park in **England**
* 🎭 **Theatreland** → London's West End, famous for its theatres and musicals
* 🏫 **School Governors** → Set the school's strategic direction and monitor its performance
* 🇺🇸 **American Independence** → British taxation of the American colonies led to the American Revolution
* ☠ **Black Death (1348)** → Reached Britain during the **Middle Ages**
* 👩 **Emmeline Pankhurst** → Leader of the Suffragettes who campaigned for women's voting rights
* 🐉 **Cardiff** → Capital city of **Wales**
* 🚢 **Great Depression (1930s)** → Britain's shipbuilding industry was severely affected
* 🏃 **Sir Roger Bannister** → First person to run a mile in under **four minutes** (1954)
* 🌙 **Eid al-Fitr** → Celebrates the end of **Ramadan**, the Muslim month of fasting
