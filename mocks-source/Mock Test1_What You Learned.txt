🧠 What You Learned

These are the key facts and topics reinforced in this mock test. Review them to strengthen your knowledge before taking another mock test.

✝ Easter → Celebrated in March or April, following Lent and Good Friday
🪙 UK Currency → Valid UK coins are 1p, 2p, 5p, 10p, 20p, 50p, £1 and £2 (there is no 25p coin)
🌱 Eden Project → Located in Cornwall, South West England
🌍 British Overseas Territories → St Helena and the Falkland Islands are linked to the UK but are not part of Great Britain or the UK
🗳 Fundamental British Values → Democracy and the Rule of Law are core principles of British life
⚖ Jury Service → Jurors are selected from the Electoral Register
⛪ Church of Scotland → A Presbyterian Church
⛳ Golf → Originated in Scotland, with St Andrews known as the "Home of Golf"
😂 April Fool's Day (1 April) → Media often publish jokes until midday
🌳 Charles II → Hid in the Royal Oak after defeat and later returned during the Restoration (1660)
👑 Henry VIII → Father of Queen Elizabeth I and founder of the Church of England
👮 Police Complaints → Can be made through several official channels, not just one organisation
☘ St Patrick → Patron Saint of Northern Ireland
💡 The Enlightenment → 18th-century movement promoting science, philosophy and political thought
👑 Mary, Queen of Scots → A Catholic, whose rivalry with Protestant Elizabeth I shaped British history
⚖ Civil Law → Covers disputes such as workplace discrimination, while criminal law deals with offences against society
⚓ Admiral Nelson → Died at the Battle of Trafalgar (1805) after defeating the French and Spanish fleets
👑 Glorious Revolution (1688) → William of Orange became king, leading to the Bill of Rights (1689)
🌍 Friends of the Earth → Environmental charity campaigning on climate change and conservation
⚖ Equality Before the Law → Marriage does not remove the requirement for consent; rape within marriage is a criminal offence
🇿🇦 Boer War (1899–1902) → Fought in South Africa between Britain and the Dutch settlers known as the Boers
🗳 Canvassing → Persuading people to vote for a political party during election campaigns
📚 J.R.R. Tolkien → The Lord of the Rings was voted Britain's best-loved novel in 2003
🌍 Second World War → Germany invaded Poland in 1939, marking the beginning of WWII
