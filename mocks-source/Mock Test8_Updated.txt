Q1. What are two responsibilities that you will have as a British citizen or permanent resident of the UK?

✅ To look after the area in which you live and the environment

✅ To look after yourself and your family

❌ To stay in the UK forever

❌ To promote work in your local community

Explanation: If you wish to be a permanent resident or citizen of the UK, you should look after yourself and your family, look after the area in which you live and the environment, respect and obey the law, and treat others with fairness.

🧠 Memory Tip

Understand what each option means:

✅ To look after the area in which you live and the environment → A responsibility expected of every citizen or permanent resident.
✅ To look after yourself and your family → A personal responsibility expected of everyone.
❌ To stay in the UK forever → Permanent residents and citizens are free to travel or live abroad; there is no requirement to remain in the UK.
❌ To promote work in your local community → Volunteering and community work are encouraged but are not official responsibilities.

Quick Memory Rule

Think:

🏠 Home → Yourself & Family

🌳 Outside → Area & Environment

Home + Environment = The TWO correct answers

Explanation: Big Ben is the nickname for the great bell of the clock at the Houses of Parliament in London.

Don't just remember where Big Ben is—remember what each place is:

🗿 Nelson's Column → Trafalgar Square

🔔 Big Ben → Houses of Parliament

Q3. When were men and women given the right to vote at the age of 21?

❌ 1918

❌ 1903

✅ 1928

❌ 1923

Explanation: In 1928, women were given the right to vote at the age of 21, the same as men.

🧠 Memory Tip

Know why each year is different:

❌ 1903 → The Women's Social and Political Union (Suffragettes) was founded. Women did not gain voting rights this year.
❌ 1918 → Women over 30 who met certain property qualifications gained the right to vote after the First World War.
❌ 1923 → No major change to voting rights.
✅ 1928 → Women gained the right to vote at 21, the same age as men.

Quick Memory Rule

📅 1918 = Women 30+

📅 1928 = Women = Men (21 years)

Remember:

1928 = Equality
